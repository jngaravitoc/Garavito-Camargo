The interaction of the Milky Way and the Large Magellanic Cloud:

Nicolas Garavito-Camargo, Gurtina Besla, Chervin F. P Laporte, Kathryn Johnston, Facundo A. Gomez and Laura L. Watkins

Here you cand find complementary figures from our paper Hunting for the Dark Matter Wake Induced by the Large Magellanic Cloud (LMC). The figures below show the perturbations caused by the LMC in the density, velocity dispersion, and mean velocities of the stellar halo of the Milky Way. The figures show spherical projections in Mollweide coordinates at different Galactocentric radii. If you find these plots useful and you happen to need the data please don't hesitate to contact me at: jngaravitoc@email.arizona.edu

The video above, shows the density enhancement $\Delta \rho = \frac{\rho}{\overline{\rho}}-1$ computed in sphericall shells centered at the center of the Milky Way's disk. $\overline{\rho}$ represents the mean density inside the spherical shell and $\rho$ is the local density.
